\section{Fourier Neural Operators}
\label{sec:fno}

Fourier Neural Operators (FNOs) were introduced in~\cite{li2020fourier} (see also~\cite{kovachki2023neuraloperatorduruisseaux2025guide}) to solve parametric partial differential equations. 
One important feature of Neural Operators is that they are designed as maps from one function space to another. 
While trained on discretisations of functions on a grid, the resulting model is grid-independent.

\subsection{General structure of Neural Operators}
Let $D\subset \R^d$ be an open domain. 
Let $\mathcal{A}=\mathcal{A}(D;\R^{d_a})$ and $\mathcal{U}=\mathcal{U}(D;\R^{d_u})$ be two Banach spaces of functions on $D$.
A Fourier Neural Operator is a map $\mathcal{F}:\mathcal{A}\to \mathcal{U}$ of the form
\begin{equation*}
  \mathcal{F} = \mathcal{Q}\circ \sigma(\mathcal{W}_L+\mathcal{K}_L)\circ \cdots \circ \sigma(\mathcal{W}_1+\mathcal{K}_1)\circ \mathcal{P},
\end{equation*}

\end{equation*}

\begin{example}
Consider the heat equation on $\R$ at a fixed time $t>0$:
\begin{equation*}
  \partial_t u = \partial_{xx} u, \quad u(x,0)=u_0(x).
\end{equation*}
The fundamental solution (heat kernel) is given by
\begin{equation*}
  G_t(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\pi t}} \exp\left(-\frac{x^2}{4t}\right).
\end{equation*}
The solution to the heat equation at time $t$ is given by
\begin{equation*}
  u(x,t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} G_t(x-y) u_0(y) dy.
\end{equation*}
The Fourier transform of the heat kernel is given by
\begin{equation*}
  \hat{G}_t(k) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\pi t}} \exp\left(-\frac{k^2}{4t}\right).
\end{equation*}
The Fourier transform of the solution to the heat equation is given by
\begin{equation*}
  \hat{u}(k,t) = \hat{u}_0(k) \hat{G}_t(k).
\end{equation*}
\end{example}
